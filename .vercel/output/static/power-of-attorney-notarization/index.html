Types of Power of Attorney in UAE

A Power of Attorney in UAE can be prepared in different formats depending on the authority you want to give another person. Some POAs provide broad legal powers, while others are limited to a single task. Choosing the correct type helps ensure the document serves its intended purpose.

📋

General Power of Attorney

A General Power of Attorney gives broad authority to an appointed person to manage multiple legal, financial, and administrative matters on your behalf. It is commonly used when someone needs ongoing assistance while living abroad or is unable to handle important responsibilities personally.

A General POA may be used for:

  • •Managing bank accounts
  • •Signing contracts
  • •Handling government formalities
  • •Representing the principal before authorities
  • •Managing personal assets
🎯

Special Power of Attorney

A Special Power of Attorney is created for one specific purpose. The authority is limited to the task mentioned in the document and cannot be used for unrelated matters.

It is commonly issued for:

  • •Selling or purchasing a property
  • •Completing a specific banking transaction
  • •Representing someone in a particular legal matter
  • •Signing a defined agreement
  • •Collecting official documents
🏠

Property Power of Attorney

A Property Power of Attorney allows another person to manage real estate transactions on behalf of the property owner. This type is often used by people living outside the UAE or those unable to attend property procedures personally.

It may be used for:

  • •Buying or selling property
  • •Leasing residential or commercial units
  • •Property registration
  • •Mortgage-related formalities
  • •Property management and maintenance
🏢

Corporate Power of Attorney

A Corporate Power of Attorney allows a company to authorize an individual to act on its behalf for business and legal matters. The powers granted depend on the company's requirements and internal approvals. Many organisations also require Power of Attorney legal services when appointing representatives for commercial transactions or regulatory procedures.

It is commonly used for:

  • •Signing commercial agreements
  • •Representing the company before government departments
  • •Managing licensing procedures
  • •Handling corporate documentation
  • •Share transfers and company resolutions
💰

Financial Power of Attorney

A Financial Power of Attorney gives someone authority to handle financial matters without giving them broader legal powers. It is suitable when the principal only wants assistance with money-related responsibilities.

It may include:

  • •Operating bank accounts
  • •Paying bills and financial obligations
  • •Managing investments
  • •Handling insurance matters
  • •Collecting payments
👨‍👩‍👧

Personal and Family Power of Attorney

This type of POA is used for personal matters involving family responsibilities or individual affairs. It allows a trusted person to manage specific personal obligations when the principal cannot be present.

It is commonly used for:

  • •Child travel authorizations
  • •Sponsorship-related formalities
  • •Managing personal affairs during overseas travel
  • •Family-related legal documentation
  • •Collecting personal records and certificates
🚗

Vehicle Power of Attorney

A Vehicle Power of Attorney authorizes another person to complete vehicle-related procedures on behalf of the owner. It is useful when buying, selling, transferring, or exporting a vehicle.

Common purposes include:

  • •Vehicle sale or purchase
  • •Ownership transfer
  • •Vehicle registration
  • •Vehicle export
  • •Related RTA formalities
📝

Limited Power of Attorney

A Limited Power of Attorney grants authority for a specific responsibility and for a defined period. Once the assigned task is completed or the validity period ends, the authority automatically comes to an end.

🛡️

Durable Power of Attorney

A Durable Power of Attorney is recognised in certain countries and continues to remain effective even if the person granting the authority later becomes unable to manage their own affairs. The acceptance of this type depends on the laws of both the issuing country and the country where it will be used.

🏥

Medical Power of Attorney

A Medical Power of Attorney allows an appointed individual to make healthcare decisions on behalf of another person when permitted under the applicable laws. The availability and legal recognition of this type of POA vary between jurisdictions and may not apply in every country.

Step-by-Step

POA Drafted Outside the UAE

When a Power of Attorney is prepared outside the UAE, it normally requires legalization before it can be accepted by UAE authorities.

The process generally follows these steps.

01

Notarization

The document is signed before a Notary Public in the country where the POA is issued.

02

Home Country Ministry of Foreign Affairs

The document is authenticated by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs or the equivalent authority in the issuing country.

03

UAE Embassy or Consulate

The UAE Embassy or Consulate verifies the previous attestations and legalizes the document for use in the UAE.

04

UAE MOFA Attestation

After the document arrives in the UAE, it must receive final attestation from the UAE Ministry of Foreign Affairs. Once this stage is completed, the Power of Attorney Attestation Dubai process is generally complete.

05

Legal Translation

When the document is prepared in a language other than Arabic, it should be translated by a legal translator approved by the UAE Ministry of Justice before submission to many authorities.

Common situations for overseas POAs

→Property transactions while living abroad
→Business representation
→Family or inheritance matters
→Banking authorizations
→Court-related procedures

POA Drafted Inside the UAE

When a POA is prepared within the UAE, fewer legalization steps are usually required.

01

Drafting

The document is prepared according to its intended legal purpose.

02

Notary or Embassy

UAE residents generally complete notarization through Dubai Courts or the Abu Dhabi Judicial Department. For a Power of Attorney Abu Dhabi, the document follows the procedures established by the Abu Dhabi Judicial Department. Foreign nationals may also prepare certain POAs through their embassy or consulate, depending on their country's requirements.

03

Electronic Notarization

Several UAE authorities now offer electronic notarization, allowing eligible documents to be signed and verified remotely. The exact process for a POA in UAE depends on the authority where the document will be submitted.

04

MOFA Attestation

When the document will be used outside the UAE, additional MOFA attestation may be required before overseas acceptance.

Common uses inside the UAE

→Property transactions
→Court representation
→Company matters
→Personal legal affairs

Requirements

Documents Required for Power of Attorney in UAE

The documents required for a Power of Attorney depend on the purpose of the POA, where it is prepared, and who is issuing it. Personal, corporate, and overseas cases often have different requirements. Preparing the correct documents from the beginning helps avoid unnecessary delays during notarization or attestation.

Individual & Corporate Requirements Covered

👤

Documents Required for Individual Power of Attorney

Individuals usually need to provide:

→Original passport and passport copy
→Emirates ID for UAE residents
→Valid UAE residence visa, where applicable
→Passport copy of the attorney (the person receiving authority)
→Draft Power of Attorney document
→Passport-size photographs, when requested by the authority
→Any supporting documents related to the purpose of the POA
🏢

Documents Required for Corporate Power of Attorney

Companies may need additional legal documents to prove that the authorized person has the right to act on behalf of the business. Commonly required documents include:

→Valid trade licence
→Memorandum of Association (MOA)
→Articles of Association (AOA), where applicable
→Board Resolution authorizing the POA
→Passport and Emirates ID of the authorized signatory
→Company stamp, when required
→Supporting commercial documents depending on the transaction

Additional Documents Based on the Type of POA

Some Power of Attorney documents require supporting evidence related to the transaction.

🏠

Property Power of Attorney

  • •Title deed or property purchase agreement
  • •Property details
  • •Mortgage documents, when applicable
🚗

Vehicle Power of Attorney

  • •Vehicle registration card
  • •Emirates Vehicle Gate or RTA documents, where required
💰

Financial Power of Attorney

  • •Bank account details
  • •Supporting financial documents requested by the bank
🏢

Corporate or Commercial Power of Attorney

  • •Commercial agreements
  • •Share transfer documents
  • •Company licences or approvals

Documents Required for POA Drafted Outside the UAE

When a Power of Attorney is prepared outside the UAE, extra legalization documents are usually required before it can be accepted. These may include:

→Original Power of Attorney
→Notary Public certification
→Home country's Ministry of Foreign Affairs attestation
→UAE Embassy or Consulate attestation
→UAE MOFA attestation after arrival in the UAE
→Certified Arabic translation, when required

The exact sequence depends on the country where the document was issued.

Documents Required for POA Drafted Inside the UAE

For a Power of Attorney prepared within the UAE, the process is generally simpler. You will normally need:

→Emirates ID or passport
→Draft Power of Attorney
→Passport details of the attorney
→Supporting documents related to the transaction
→Additional approvals for corporate or property matters, where applicable

Some UAE authorities also offer electronic notarization, allowing documents to be completed online after identity verification.

Legal Translation Requirements

Many government departments and courts require documents in Arabic. Documents prepared in another language should be translated by a legal translator approved by the UAE Ministry of Justice.

The translated document must accurately match the original version before it can be submitted for official use.

⚠️

Common Reasons for Document Rejection

Many delays happen because of simple documentation errors. The most common issues include:

•Passport names that do not match the POA
•Expired identification documents
•Missing embassy or MOFA stamps
•Incorrect or incomplete legal translation
•Missing supporting documents
•Unsigned or improperly notarized POA
•Using an outdated document format

Reviewing every detail carefully helps prevent delays when submitting a POA UAE application.
