Service Overview

Why Attest Your Birth Certificate?

Index Office Service provide you a quick and effortless service with our experts and we have 25 years of experience in the field of certificate attestation. In UAE, birth certificates must undergo birth certificate attestation as a critical step in the legalisation and validation of their credibility.

To protect the legitimacy and integrity of all foreign papers inside the UAE, the government has made attestation essential. The attestation of birth certificates is essential for many governmental and private processes, including getting a visa, requesting admission to a school, sponsoring dependents, and conducting business in the United Arab Emirates.

What is a birth certificate?

A birth certificate is an official record of a person’s birth information. It is often provided immediately after a child is born by a government entity, like a vital statistics office or registrar’s office. A person’s identity, age, and parentage may all be legally proven with the use of a birth certificate.

Legal Process Guide

Issuing & Acquiring Birth Certificates in UAE

How to Lawfully Issue a Birth Certificate in UAE

01

Hospital Notification

Hospital staff notifies the relevant authorities immediately about the birth.

02

Register the Birth

Visit Public Health or Preventive Medicine Department within 30 days. Provide original & photocopy of child's hospital birth notification.

03

Pay the Fees

Pay required nominal registration fees at the designated counter.

04

Collection

Once verified, the authorities issue the birth certificate. Visit the office with the receipt to collect.

How to Acquire a Legally Recognized Birth Certificate

01

Medical Alert

Confirm hospital staff alerts appropriate UAE public health authorities regarding the baby's birth.

02

Submit Required Documents

Provide parents' passports + Emirates ID copies, original hospital notification, and marriage licence (if necessary).

03

Verification of Documents

Government authorities check all supplied documentation for absolute correctness and completeness.

04

Biometric Verification

Provide biometric details such as parent fingerprints and photos for identity validation purposes.

The duration of birth certificate attestation depends on factors such as the country where the document was issued, the authorities involved, and whether additional verification is required. Index AUH carefully reviews each case and manages the process efficiently to avoid unnecessary delays.

If the birth certificate is not issued in English or Arabic, certified translation may be required for acceptance by UAE authorities. Index AUH will confirm whether translation is necessary and assist you in arranging approved translation services if needed.
