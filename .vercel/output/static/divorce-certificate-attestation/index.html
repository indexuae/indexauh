Step by Step

Divorce Certificate Attestation Process for UAE

Authorities follow several verification steps before accepting the document in the UAE. Each step confirms authenticity.

01

Notary Verification

The issuing country verifies the original document through a notary authority.

02

Home Department Attestation

State authorities review the document and confirm the notary verification.

03

Ministry of Foreign Affairs in Home Country

The foreign affairs department checks the document before it moves to embassy level.

04

UAE Embassy Attestation

The UAE embassy in the issuing country confirms the document for UAE use.

05

MOFA Attestation in UAE

Finally, the UAE Ministry of Foreign Affairs completes the verification. At this stage the document becomes valid for official use.

Plan Ahead

Processing Time and Cost

Processing time depends on several factors, and each document follows a verification path before approval in the UAE. Attestation cost also depends on several elements, since every stage in the verification process has its own government charge.

How Long Does It Take?

  • Country that issued the divorce certificate
  • Verification required from local authorities
  • UAE Embassy approval in the issuing country
  • MOFA attestation in the UAE
  • Legal translation requirements for non-Arabic documents
  • Public holidays in government departments
  • Accuracy of submitted documents

What Affects the Cost?

  • Government verification fees in the issuing country
  • UAE Embassy attestation charges
  • Ministry of Foreign Affairs approval in the UAE
  • Translation fees for non-Arabic documents
  • Courier or document handling services
  • Urgent processing requests
  • Number of verification stages required

Most cases finish within a few days or a few weeks, though some documents require additional verification that can increase the timeline. Documents issued outside the UAE go through more verification stages, so the total cost becomes higher compared to local documents. Common Challenges During Attestation

Many applicants face delays because of small document mistakes. These issues often appear during the verification stage.

! Name mismatch between passport and certificate
! Missing verification from the issuing authority
! Incorrect document format
! Certificates issued from unrecognized institutions
! Translation required for foreign language documents
! Incomplete document submission
! Missing passport or visa copies
! Damage or unclear printing on the certificate

Even a small error can stop the process, and authorities may return the document for correction, which increases the waiting time. Proper guidance becomes important during attestation, as professional handling helps avoid these problems and keeps the process smooth.
