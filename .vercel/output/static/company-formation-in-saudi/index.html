Strategic Location

Located in the heart of the Middle East, Saudi Arabia serves as a gateway to regional and international markets, making it an ideal location for trade and investment.

Infrastructure Development

Procedures for Forming a Company in Saudi Arabia

  1. Determine Business Activity: Identify the type of business activity and legal structure suitable for your company.
  2. Choose a Business Name: Select a unique name for your company and ensure it complies with the regulations.
  3. Draft Articles of Association: Prepare the legal documents outlining the company's structure, shareholding, and operations.
  4. Capital Requirement: Deposit the required share capital in a bank account in Saudi Arabia.
  5. Obtain Approvals and Licenses: Obtain necessary approvals and licenses from relevant government authorities, such as the Ministry of Commerce, SAGIA, and other sector-specific regulators.
  6. Commercial Registration: Register the company with the Ministry of Commerce and obtain a commercial registration (CR) certificate.
  7. Municipal License: Obtain a license from the municipality in the specific region where the company will operate.
  8. Hire Employees and Obtain Visas: Recruit employees and apply for the required visas and work permits.

Before that, you need to consider the following legal requirements such as foreign investment regulations, employment laws, ownership restrictions, and much more.
