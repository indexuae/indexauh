Why Do You Need Commercial Document Attestation?

Business documents from another country are not automatically valid in the UAE. Authorities must confirm that the documents are real and properly issued before approving your business activities.

When you plan to open a branch office, register a company, sign an international contract, or clear goods through customs, you will need Commercial Document Attestation. This confirms that your documents are genuine and issued by the right authority in your home country.

Authentication Workflow

Commercial Document Attestation Process in UAE

Commercial Document Attestation involves multiple verification levels. The process depends on where the document was issued and how it will be used.

01

Document Review and Pre-Verification

We examine the document carefully. Our team confirms stamp sequence, signature clarity, and authority requirements before submission.

02

Notarization

Some documents require notarization before further attestation. This confirms authenticity at the local level.

03

Chamber of Commerce Attestation

Trade documents such as commercial invoices must be verified by the Chamber of Commerce. Incorrect chamber stamps are a common cause of rejection.

04

Ministry of Foreign Affairs in Issuing Country

Foreign issued documents usually require attestation from the Ministry of Foreign Affairs in that country.

05

UAE Embassy Attestation

The UAE Embassy in the issuing country legalizes the document. Procedures vary depending on the country. We handle embassy coordination for 100+ countries.

06

UAE MOFA Attestation

Final verification happens at the UAE Ministry of Foreign Affairs. This seal confirms the document is legally accepted in the UAE.
