Service Overview

What is UAE MOFA Attestation?

The official authentication of documents by the UAE Ministry of Foreign Affairs (MOFA) is known as MOFA Attestation. It is the last and most important step of document attestation in the UAE, verifying that a document is authentic, lawful, and accepted by UAE government officials.

Depending on their intended use, MOFA attestation is applicable to commercial, personal, and educational certificate attestation issued both inside and outside the United Arab Emirates. Document Categories

Types of Documents Requiring UAE MOFA Attestation

MOFA attestation covers educational, personal, and commercial documents, each with distinct purposes and use cases.

Educational Documents

Educational certificates are required for employment, higher education, professional licensing, and visa purposes.

Examples include:

• Degree Certificates
• Diploma Certificates
• Mark Sheets & Transcripts
• School Leaving Certificates
• Professional Qualification Certificates

Common use: Employment visa, Golden Visa (skilled professionals), equivalency certificates.

Personal Documents

Personal documents establish identity, family relationships, and civil status.

Examples include:

• Birth Certificate
• Marriage Certificate
• Divorce Certificate
• Death Certificate
• Police Clearance Certificate (PCC)
• Medical Certificates

Common use: Family visa, spouse sponsorship, dependent visa, legal matters.

Commercial Documents

Commercial documents are required for business operations, trade, and corporate compliance.

Examples include:

• Power of Attorney (POA)
• Memorandum of Association (MOA)
• Articles of Association (AOA)
• Commercial License
• Commercial Invoices
• Board Resolutions
• Company Agreements

Common use: Business setup UAE, trade licensing, bank approvals, legal transactions.

Authentication Workflow

Procedure for Ministry of Foreign Affairs Attestation

The MOFA attestation process depends on where the document was issued.

For Documents Issued Outside the UAE

01

Issuing Country Attestation

Attestation from the relevant authorities in the country where the document was issued.

02

Foreign Affairs Attestation

Attestation from that country's Ministry of Foreign Affairs.

03

UAE Embassy Attestation

UAE Embassy attestation in the issuing country.

04

Final MOFA Attestation

Final MOFA attestation completed inside the UAE.

For Documents Issued Inside the UAE

01

Initial UAE Authority Attestation

Initial attestation from the relevant UAE authority that issued or holds the document.

02

Direct MOFA Attestation

Direct MOFA attestation completed without the need for embassy-level steps.

Why It Matters

Why Do You Need UAE MOFA Attestation?

MOFA attestation is mandatory when documents are submitted to UAE authorities for official, legal, or immigration purposes. Without MOFA attestation, documents are often rejected by government departments, employers, courts, and immigration authorities.

You need MOFA attestation for:

→ Employment in the UAE
→ Golden Visa applications
→ Residence visa processing
→ Family visa & spouse sponsorship
→ Higher education admissions
→ Business setup and trade licensing
→ Legal and court-related matters

Digital MOFA Attestation UAE

What is UAE's Digital MOFA Attestation?

The UAE has introduced a Digital Ministry of Foreign Affairs Attestation System, replacing traditional physical stamps with secure electronic attestation certificates.

This digital system allows documents to be verified online instantly, ensuring higher security, faster processing, and global recognition.

Key Features:

✔ Tamper-proof digital certificate
✔ Online verification using QR code or reference number
✔ Faster processing time
✔ No physical stamping required
✔ Accepted by UAE government entities and international institutions

How to Verify Your UAE Digital Attestation Online?

You can verify your MOFA-attested document by:

01

Visit the Portal

Visiting the official MOFA UAE verification portal.

02

Enter Reference

Entering the attestation reference number or scanning the QR code.

03

View Status

Viewing real-time authentication status.

This ensures transparency and prevents document forgery.
