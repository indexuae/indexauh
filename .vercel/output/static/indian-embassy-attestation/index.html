Step-by-Step

Step by Step Embassy Attestation Process for Indian Issued Documents in UAE

01

Document Verification

Check that your document is original and issued by a recognized authority in India. Ensure all details are correct and legible.

02

Attestation in India (If Required)

Certain documents need state-level approval. Ministry of External Affairs (MEA) attestation may also be needed, confirming your document is genuine before embassy submission.

03

Indian Embassy or Consulate Attestation in UAE

Submit your document to the Indian Embassy in Abu Dhabi or Consulate in Dubai. Embassy staff verify the authenticity and place an official stamp.

04

UAE MOFA Attestation

The Ministry of Foreign Affairs in UAE provides final approval, making your document legally valid for use with UAE authorities.

05

Secure Document Delivery

After all approvals, your attested document is returned safely, ready for visas, legal, property, employment, or business purposes.

Indian Embassy and Indian Consulate Difference

The Indian Embassy is located in Abu Dhabi. The Indian Consulate operates from Dubai. Indian embassy attestation is handled through the correct authority based on document type and location. We manage submissions through the right channel to avoid rejection.

Indian Embassy Marriage Certificate Attestation Process

Marriage certificate attestation is usually needed for family visa, spouse sponsorship, or immigration in the UAE.

1.

Marriage Certificate Verification

We check that the original marriage certificate is correct and complete, confirm it is issued by the proper Indian authority, and make sure the language is readable and clear.

2.

Attestation in India

Some marriage certificates need approval from the State or Home Department in India, then verification by the Ministry of External Affairs (MEA), confirming authenticity before sending to the UAE.

3.

Indian Embassy or Consulate Attestation in UAE

Submit your certificate to the Indian Embassy in Abu Dhabi or the Consulate in Dubai. The embassy staff checks your certificate and places an official stamp.

4.

UAE MOFA Attestation

The Ministry of Foreign Affairs in the UAE gives the final approval, making your marriage certificate legally accepted by UAE authorities.

Indian Embassy Power of Attorney Attestation Process

A Power of Attorney (POA) grants another person authority to act for you, and must be handled carefully to be valid in the UAE.

1.

Draft Review

Check that the purpose of the POA is clear and the wording is correct so it works legally.

2.

Signing and Notarisation

The document must be signed properly, and the identity of the signer is verified to avoid problems later.

3.

Attestation in India

Some POAs need approval from local authorities in India. The Ministry of External Affairs (MEA) also verifies the document.

4.

Submit for Indian Embassy/Consulate Verification in UAE

The POA must be submitted to the Indian Embassy in Abu Dhabi or the Consulate in Dubai, where officials confirm its authenticity and stamp the document.

5.

UAE MOFA Attestation

The Ministry of Foreign Affairs in UAE provides final approval. Once done, the POA is legally valid for property, banking, or business use in the UAE.
