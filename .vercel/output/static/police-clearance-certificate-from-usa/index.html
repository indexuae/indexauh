Overview

What is a Police Clearance Certificate in the USA?

In the USA, a Police Clearance Certificate (PCC) is an official document issued by law enforcement agencies to verify an individual's criminal record or lack thereof within the country. It serves as evidence of an individual's criminal history or the absence of any criminal records.

Obtaining a PCC in the USA is important for various purposes. Many employers require it as part of their background check process. When applying for visas, immigration, or residency in other countries, a PCC from the USA may be required to assess an individual's criminal background.

A PCC is also often necessary for adoptions, educational institutions, professional licensing, and certain legal processes. Index specializes in helping individuals navigate the entire PCC application process with ease and confidence.

Requirements

Documents Required to Get PCC in the USA

Fingerprint Card

Applicants need to provide their fingerprints for a background check, which can be done through a local law enforcement agency or an authorized service provider.

Application Form

An official application form provided by the law enforcement agency or the state's criminal records office must be accurately completed.

Identification Documents

Valid identification, such as a passport, driver's license, or social security card, must be submitted along with your application.

Fees

Applicants are usually required to pay the prescribed fee, which varies depending on the jurisdiction and the type of PCC requested.
